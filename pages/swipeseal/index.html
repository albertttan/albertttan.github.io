Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about SwipeSeal

SwipeSeal is designed to be compatible with a wide range of bioactive compounds, including hydrogels, cell suspensions, protein solutions, and synthetic biomaterials. Our non-reactive polymer housing ensures no contamination or degradation of sensitive biological materials.

The SwipeSeal applicator itself has a shelf life of 3 years. The bioactive compounds it dispenses will have their own shelf life according to their specifications, which SwipeSeal is designed to preserve through its non-reactive materials and airtight sealing mechanism.

Yes, SwipeSeal is autoclavable and designed for use in sterile environments. The materials used in its construction can withstand standard sterilization procedures including ethylene oxide, gamma radiation, and autoclave cycles up to 134°C.

The standard SwipeSeal applicator has a capacity of 5ml, with options for both smaller (2ml) and larger (10ml) cartridges depending on the application needs. The modular design allows for quick cartridge replacement during procedures.

SwipeSeal is designed with an intuitive interface that requires minimal training. However, we provide comprehensive documentation and video tutorials with each sample. For research institutions, we can also arrange virtual training sessions upon request.
