Environmental News of the Day 每日环境新闻

Millions of gallons of untreated sewage were illegally pumped into Windermere, England's largest lake, for 10 hours in February 2024, after a telecommunications failure caused the main pumps at a sewage treatment plant to shut down.

The sewage discharge was illegal because it occurred when the pumping station was not overwhelmed by rainfall or snowmelt, the only conditions under which it is permitted to discharge untreated sewage into the lake.

Windermere has suffered from algal blooms in recent summers, due in part to phosphorus from treated and untreated sewage discharges, further threatening this UNESCO World Heritage Site and popular tourist attraction.

Unsuccessful attempts to investigate such incidents have threatened England's lake ecosystem, highlighting the need for stricter regulations and increased compliance checks by the Environment Agency.

We Have Not Long to Love
Tenessee Williams

We have not long to love.
Light does not stay.
The tender things are those
we fold away.
Coarse fabrics are the ones
for common wear.
In silence I have watched you
comb your hair.
Intimate the silence,
dim and warm.
I could but did not, reach
to touch your arm.
I could, but do not, break
that which is still.
(Almost the faintest whisper
would be shrill.)
So moments pass as though
they wished to stay.
We have not long to love.
A night. A day....
